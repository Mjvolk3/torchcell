\section{Results\cb{target ~1,900 words}}\label{sec:results}

\bmhead{A shared ontology and knowledge graph unify metabolic-engineering data\cb{~380 words}}
\textit{[FILLER -- R1.]} TorchCell annotates literature and public datasets with a
shared experiment ontology and ingests them into a Neo4j knowledge graph;
dataloaders emit deep-learning-ready datasets in which each strain is a
perturbation operator over a shared wildtype reference cell
(Fig.~\ref{fig:torchcell}; data scale in Suppl.\ Fig.\ S2; ontology/KG spec in
Suppl.\ Fig.\ S4).

\bmhead{The Cell Graph Transformer predicts trigenic gene-gene interactions at state of the art\cb{~380 words}}
\textit{[FILLER -- R2.]} The Cell Graph Transformer (CGT; architecture in
Fig.~\ref{fig:torchcell}c and Methods) constrains attention with biological interaction
graphs and maps the reference cell through an equivariant perturbation operator. On
trigenic gene-gene interactions (GGI), where classical ML fails (Suppl.\ Fig.\ S1), CGT
reaches Pearson $r = 0.454$ (Spearman $0.421$), outperforming Yeast9 FBA, DCell, and DANGO
(Fig.~\ref{fig:ggi}).

\bmhead{One embedding, many phenotypes: expression, morphology, and fitness\cb{~380 words}}
\textit{[FILLER -- R3.]} The same embedding predicts knockout expression ($r = 0.543$),
single-knockout morphology ($r = 0.619$), and fitness (Fig.~\ref{fig:multitask};
expression diagnostics in Suppl.\ Fig.\ S5).

\bmhead{Extending to metabolism and strain design\cb{~380 words}}
\textit{[FILLER -- R5.]} Grounding the cell representation in metabolism, CGT recommends gene
targets for production phenotypes and pairs with the UIUC iBioFoundry for iterative design
(Fig.~\ref{fig:metabolism}; inference at scale in Suppl.\ Fig.\ S6).
