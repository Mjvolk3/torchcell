## 2026.07.20 - Baseline SMF Characterization of the Strains on the Plate

Exists so the 10 genes the wet-lab actually built have a published single-mutant-fitness
baseline to measure against — the existing panel-12 figures cover a different gene set
(they include the two dropped genes), so reading them would mis-state the baseline for
this plate. Emits two views of the same data: a between-source forest
(Costanzo2016 / Kuzmin2018 / Kuzmin2020, fitness ± SD) and a Gaussian ridgeline on a
shared axis so per-gene spread (σ) is comparable gene-to-gene.

Costanzo fitness range 0.590–1.085, mean σ = 0.087. Only **CBF1/YJR060W** (0.590) is a
real defect; the other nine sit near WT = 1.0, with RPS9A/YPL081W the widest (σ = 0.155).
That near-neutral baseline is the point: it means any departure the assay measures on
construction is signal rather than a pre-existing single-mutant effect.
